Rube Goldberg Engineering Project

This project was from the AVID course I took in the seventh grade of Fallon Middle School. The purpose of taking this course was to learn about career options and what you gain from them as well as college preparation. The project was to expand engineering creativity and design. The objective was to build a unique chain of actions to solve a simple task, which was many of Rube Goldberg's ideas. I worked with Charles Abagat (shown in the image) and other partners to assemble a complicated machine to simply pour a glass of water into a cup. As our final product, we used wood blocks as dominoes to eventually knock a black marble which would then hit the Hotwheel Toy car. The car would then land on a moustrap tied to a string. As the mousetrap goes off it would pull the string that is taped to a cup filled with water, and the cup would fall over and spill the water into the actual cup. This complex design was not easy to accomplish, there were some challenges our group faced. The mousetrap broke twice, and we almost had to cancel using it. Also, it was very difficult to get the marble to hit car, let alone make it fall off to land on the mousetrap. After a lot of tests, we were able to successfully pour a cup of water. This project took approximately a week and a half.

Image Manipulation Python Project

The Image Manipulation Project was also completed during my Computer Science Principles course freshmen year. The purpose of this project was to expand and apply my knowledge of image manipulation in Python. This project was done with my partner, Advaith Srinivasan, and we started 3/4 and finished 3/24. We were provided four scenarios, each scenario discussing a certain topic or theme to make your modified and manipulated images for. We decided to make our images based off the third scenario, which is about a company who wants to have images that show their client that they have a professional workforce. After a day of our brainstorming, we chose three images to take and gradually modify to our needs. Project Notebook is here. Our Presentation for the project is here. Each image was required to have a border or silhouette according the company/client's needs, and it had to reflect on the company itself, so for all three images there was a deeper meaning behind it. The first image is supposed to represent a single employees creative thought process, the second image is supposed to show the limitless energy in one workplace, and finally the third image is supposed to represent the company's constant resolving of situations no matter the challenge, and how unique the situation is. A lot of the challenges me and my partner had to overcome was broken code and errors in code.
